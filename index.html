Finding 2 · The paper trail begins

The fiber permit names the tower

Troy's "Permits Issued" database contains Metro Act ROW permit PROW2023-347, issued 2023-10-05, applicant Jeff Davis — a recurring AT&T fiber contractor:

"Directional bore and place 2" PC from the pole at Wattles and Northfield Pkwy south for approximately 1950'. Set 2 new hand holes in ROW. To provide service to new proposed AT&T mobility cell site."

This is the fiber backhaul for the tower — the first public document that ties AT&T infrastructure to this exact location.

Finding 3 · What was approved

What Resolution 21-064 actually authorizes

  • A ground lease to New Cingular Wireless PCS, LLC (AT&T) of ~2,500 sq ft at the north (wooded) end of the Boulan Park Middle School site.
  • The Superintendent (or designee) is authorized to negotiate, finalize, and execute the ground lease and all incidental documents.
  • The packet leaned heavily on RF-safety framing — it included American Cancer Society notes, RF-radiation articles, "5G — no adverse health effects" material, plus a photo-simulation and propagation maps.

The resolution approves the lease in principle and hands execution to administration — the negotiated terms live in an "Attachment B" that travels separately.

Open question · The money

How much is the District paid? Not in the public record

  • The resolution references "Attachment B — General Parameters of Lease Agreement," but that attachment was never uploaded to BoardDocs — only the placeholder word "Attached" appears.
  • No dollar figure appears anywhere in the public packet, the lease presentation, or the meeting minutes (which do itemize other dollar amounts that night).
  • The financial terms were most likely settled in the 2021-06-15 closed session — whose minutes file is sealed / returns 404.

→ The revenue figure is a FOIA target: the executed AT&T ground lease, Attachment B to Res. 21-064, and the closed-session minutes.

Finding 2 · The legal frame

Why a tower next to a school is legal

Telecom Act of 1996 · §704

47 U.S.C. §332(c)(7) — local government may not deny or regulate a wireless facility over RF-emission health concerns if it meets FCC limits.

FCC "shot clocks"

Localities must act within fixed deadlines — miss them and the application is deemed granted.

Michigan MCL 125.3514

Wireless siting law — collocations are by-right; new support structures get special-land-use review on a ~90-day clock.

Federal preemption does most of the work. The school district's own decision — leasing the ground — is the part that was discretionary.
